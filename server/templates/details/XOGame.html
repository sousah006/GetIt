XO-Game (Tic-Tac-Toe)

"XO-Game, also known as Tic-Tac-Toe, is a classic pencil-and-paper game for two players. The game is played on a 3x3 grid where players take turns marking 'X' or 'O' in empty squares. The player who succeeds in placing three of their marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row wins the game."

How to Play:

  • Player 1 starts by placing an 'X' in an empty square.
  • Player 2 then places an 'O' in a different empty square.
  • Players alternate turns until one player achieves three marks in a row or no more moves are possible, resulting in a draw.

History:

The origins of Tic-Tac-Toe trace back to ancient Egypt, where similar games using markings on a grid have been found. It gained popularity in the 20th century and remains a beloved game for its simplicity and strategic depth.
